RISK ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
Enterprise Risk Management for Insurance Operations
Updated: January 2024

PART 1: RISK ASSESSMENT FUNDAMENTALS

1.1 DEFINITION AND PURPOSE
Risk assessment is the systematic process of identifying, analyzing, and evaluating risks to determine appropriate mitigation strategies and pricing.

Key objectives:
- Accurate pricing of insurance products
- Identification of high-risk exposures
- Portfolio diversification
- Capital allocation optimization
- Regulatory compliance

1.2 RISK CATEGORIES
Underwriting Risk: Risk of inadequate premium for exposure
Market Risk: Investment portfolio volatility
Credit Risk: Reinsurer or counterparty default
Operational Risk: Internal process failures
Catastrophe Risk: Large-scale natural disasters
Regulatory Risk: Compliance and legal changes
Reputational Risk: Brand damage from incidents

PART 2: AUTO INSURANCE RISK ASSESSMENT

2.1 DRIVER RISK FACTORS
Age and experience:
- Teen drivers (16-19): 300% higher accident rate
- Young adults (20-24): 150% higher accident rate
- Senior drivers (70+): 75% higher accident rate
- Optimal age range: 30-60 years

Driving record:
- At-fault accident: 40% premium increase
- Speeding ticket: 15-25% increase
- DUI: 80-120% increase or decline coverage
- License suspension: Immediate declination

Annual mileage:
- Under 5,000 miles: Low risk
- 5,000-12,000 miles: Average risk
- 12,000-20,000 miles: Elevated risk
- Over 20,000 miles: High risk (commercial consideration)

2.2 VEHICLE RISK FACTORS
Safety ratings:
- Top safety pick (IIHS): 10% discount
- 5-star crash rating: 5% discount
- Advanced safety features: Additional 5-10% discount

Theft risk:
- High-theft models: 15-30% surcharge
- Anti-theft devices: 5% discount
- GPS tracking: Additional 5% discount

Performance classification:
- Sports cars: 25-50% surcharge
- High-horsepower vehicles: 20% surcharge
- Standard sedans: Base rate
- Minivans/family SUVs: 5-10% discount

2.3 GEOGRAPHIC RISK
Urban vs. rural:
- Dense urban areas: 40% higher rates (more accidents, theft)
- Suburban areas: Base rates
- Rural areas: 15% lower rates (lower frequency)

Weather risk:
- Hail-prone regions: 10% surcharge
- Flood zones: Comprehensive rate increase
- Ice/snow regions: 5-10% surcharge

Crime statistics:
- High auto theft rate: 15-25% surcharge
- Vandalism rates: 5-15% impact
- Low crime areas: 5-10% discount

PART 3: HOMEOWNERS RISK ASSESSMENT

3.1 PROPERTY CHARACTERISTICS
Construction type:
- Frame construction: Base rate
- Masonry: 10% discount
- Fire-resistive: 15% discount
- Log/unusual construction: 25% surcharge

Age of home:
- New construction (0-5 years): 15% discount
- 6-20 years: Base rate
- 21-40 years: 10% surcharge
- 41+ years: 20% surcharge (may require inspection)

Square footage and value:
- Under $200,000: Standard rates
- $200,000-$500,000: Base rates with full coverage
- $500,000-$1,000,000: Premium tier with enhanced coverage
- Over $1,000,000: High-value tier with specialized coverage

3.2 LOCATION HAZARDS
Wildfire risk:
- High hazard zones: 50-100% surcharge or decline
- Moderate hazard: 25% surcharge
- Low hazard: Base rate
- Defensible space mitigation: Up to 20% credit

Flood risk:
- FEMA Zone A/V: Require separate flood policy
- FEMA Zone X (moderate): Encourage flood coverage
- Minimal risk areas: Base rate

Hurricane/wind exposure:
- Coastal properties: 30-60% surcharge
- Wind mitigation features: 15-25% credit
- Hurricane shutters/impact glass: Additional 10% credit

Earthquake risk:
- High seismic zones: Require separate earthquake policy
- Moderate zones: Offer earthquake endorsement
- Low risk areas: Base rate

3.3 LOSS HISTORY
Property-specific:
- No claims in 5 years: 15% discount
- 1 claim in 3 years: Base rate
- 2 claims in 3 years: 25% surcharge
- 3+ claims in 3 years: Decline coverage

Neighborhood loss ratio:
- Review ZIP code loss experience
- High-loss ZIPs: 10-20% surcharge
- Low-loss ZIPs: 5-10% discount

PART 4: CATASTROPHE MODELING

4.1 NATURAL DISASTER EXPOSURE
Hurricane modeling:
- Analyze historical hurricane tracks
- Model potential damage based on wind speed
- Assess storm surge impact
- Calculate probable maximum loss (PML)

Earthquake modeling:
- Fault line proximity
- Soil liquefaction potential
- Building construction standards
- Retrofit status

Wildfire modeling:
- Vegetation density
- Slope and terrain
- Distance to fire station
- Fire history in area

4.2 PORTFOLIO CONCENTRATION
Maximum exposure limits by region:
- Single ZIP code: No more than 5% of total exposure
- Single county: No more than 15% of total exposure
- High-risk states: No more than 30% of portfolio
- Catastrophe-prone areas: Monitor PML closely

4.3 REINSURANCE STRATEGY
Catastrophe reinsurance:
- Cover losses exceeding $10M per event
- Annual aggregate limit: $500M
- Retention: $10M
- Multiple layers for mega-catastrophes

PART 5: CREDIT AND FINANCIAL RISK

5.1 CREDIT-BASED INSURANCE SCORES
Strong correlation between credit score and loss frequency:
- Excellent credit (750+): 40% lower loss ratio
- Good credit (650-749): Average loss ratio
- Fair credit (550-649): 15% higher loss ratio
- Poor credit (<550): 50% higher loss ratio

5.2 FINANCIAL STABILITY INDICATORS
Red flags:
- Recent bankruptcy
- Tax liens
- Multiple late payments
- High debt-to-income ratio
- Foreclosure proceedings

Positive indicators:
- Stable employment (5+ years)
- Home ownership
- Long-term insurance history
- Strong payment history

PART 6: PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS

6.1 MACHINE LEARNING MODELS
Implement predictive models for:
- Claim frequency prediction
- Claim severity estimation
- Fraud probability scoring
- Churn/retention likelihood
- Cross-sell opportunities

Model inputs:
- Historical claim data
- Demographic information
- Telematics data (if available)
- Geographic data
- Credit information
- Market trends

6.2 TELEMATICS AND USAGE-BASED INSURANCE
Data collected:
- Miles driven
- Time of day driving
- Hard braking frequency
- Rapid acceleration
- Cornering behavior
- Speed relative to posted limits

Risk scoring:
- Excellent driver: 25% discount
- Good driver: 10-15% discount
- Average driver: Base rate
- Risky driver: 10-20% surcharge

PART 7: PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT

7.1 LOSS RATIO TARGETS
Target combined ratio: 95-100%
- Loss ratio target: 65-70%
- Expense ratio target: 25-30%
- Underwriting profit: 0-5%

Acceptable ranges by line:
- Personal auto: 68-72% loss ratio
- Homeowners: 55-65% loss ratio
- Umbrella: 50-60% loss ratio

7.2 RATE ADEQUACY MONITORING
Quarterly review of:
- Actual vs. expected loss ratio
- Severity trends
- Frequency trends
- Expense ratios
- Competitive positioning

Rate adjustment triggers:
- Loss ratio exceeds target by 5%+ for 2 consecutive quarters
- Catastrophe losses exceed budget
- Competitive pressure threatens market share
- Regulatory changes impact costs

PART 8: EMERGING RISKS

8.1 CLIMATE CHANGE IMPACTS
Increasing frequency and severity of:
- Hurricanes and tropical storms
- Wildfires
- Flooding
- Hailstorms
- Extreme temperatures

Mitigation strategies:
- Update catastrophe models annually
- Adjust coastal exposure limits
- Incentivize resilient construction
- Encourage adaptation measures

8.2 TECHNOLOGY RISKS
Autonomous vehicles:
- Shift from driver liability to manufacturer liability
- Reduced frequency but higher severity
- Need for cyber coverage

Smart homes:
- IoT device vulnerabilities
- Data privacy concerns
- Potential for remote monitoring benefits

Cryptocurrency:
- Valuation challenges
- Theft and fraud concerns
- Special coverage considerations

8.3 SOCIAL INFLATION
Trends impacting claim costs:
- Larger jury awards
- Litigation funding growth
- Nuclear verdicts (>$10M)
- Attorney advertising
- Changing social attitudes

Response strategies:
- Increase liability limits
- Enhance legal defense
- Improve claims handling
- Consider excess coverage

PART 9: RISK MITIGATION

9.1 LOSS CONTROL SERVICES
Offer policyholders:
- Home safety inspections
- Risk assessment consultations
- Defensive driving courses
- Fire prevention education
- Security system recommendations

9.2 POLICY TERMS AND CONDITIONS
Risk transfer through:
- Appropriate deductibles
- Coverage limits aligned with exposure
- Exclusions for uninsurable risks
- Sub-limits for high-risk items
- Conditional coverage for certain perils

PART 10: MONITORING AND REPORTING

10.1 KEY RISK INDICATORS (KRIs)
Track monthly:
- Loss ratio by line of business
- Claim frequency and severity
- New business vs. renewal ratio
- Policy cancellation rates
- Expense ratios
- Reinsurance costs
- Reserve adequacy

10.2 RISK REPORTING
Monthly to management:
- Risk dashboard with KRIs
- Emerging risk alerts
- Catastrophe exposure report
- Large loss summaries
- Rate adequacy analysis

Quarterly to board:
- Enterprise risk profile
- Capital adequacy assessment
- Strategic risk review
- Regulatory compliance status

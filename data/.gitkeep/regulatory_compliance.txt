REGULATORY COMPLIANCE GUIDE
Insurance Operations and Requirements
Version 4.0 - December 2023

CHAPTER 1: STATE INSURANCE REGULATIONS

1.1 LICENSING AND AUTHORITY
Certificate of Authority:
- Must be licensed in each state of operation
- Annual renewal required
- Financial statements filed quarterly
- Maintain minimum capital and surplus requirements

Agent licensing:
- Individual licenses required in each state
- Continuing education: 20-24 hours every 2 years
- Background checks required
- Errors and omissions insurance mandatory

1.2 RATE AND FORM FILING
Prior approval states (35 states):
- Submit rates and forms to insurance department
- Cannot use until approved (30-90 days)
- Actuarial justification required
- Rate changes limited to once per year in some states

File and use states (10 states):
- File with insurance department
- Can implement immediately
- Subject to post-implementation review
- Must provide supporting documentation

Use and file states (5 states):
- Implement rates
- File within specified time period
- Less regulatory oversight
- Still subject to examination

1.3 PROHIBITED PRACTICES
Cannot discriminate based on:
- Race or ethnicity
- Religion
- National origin
- Gender (in some states)
- Sexual orientation (in some states)
- Marital status (in some states)

Allowed risk factors:
- Credit score (except CA, HI, MA, MD)
- Driving record
- Claims history
- Geographic location
- Property characteristics

CHAPTER 2: UNFAIR CLAIMS SETTLEMENT PRACTICES ACT

2.1 PROHIBITED CONDUCT
Insurers must not:
- Misrepresent policy provisions
- Fail to acknowledge claims promptly
- Fail to investigate claims properly
- Fail to affirm or deny coverage promptly
- Delay payments unreasonably
- Offer substantially less than amounts due
- Require unnecessary documentation
- Make claim settlement conditional on giving up other rights

2.2 PROMPT PAYMENT REQUIREMENTS
Timeframes vary by state, typical requirements:
- Acknowledge claim: Within 15 days
- Begin investigation: Within 30 days
- Make coverage decision: Within 45 days
- Issue payment: Within 30 days of settlement

Penalties for violations:
- Interest on delayed payments (6-18% annually)
- Attorney fees for policyholder
- Bad faith damages (2-3x actual damages)
- Civil penalties ($1,000-$25,000 per violation)
- License suspension or revocation

2.3 CLAIM DENIAL REQUIREMENTS
Written denial must include:
- Specific policy provision supporting denial
- Clear explanation in plain language
- Reference to claim documentation reviewed
- Information on appeal process
- Contact information for insurance department

CHAPTER 3: PRIVACY AND DATA SECURITY

3.1 GRAMM-LEACH-BLILEY ACT (GLBA)
Financial Privacy Rule:
- Provide privacy notice at policy inception
- Annual privacy notice required
- Allow opt-out of information sharing
- Limit information sharing to necessary purposes

Safeguards Rule:
- Implement comprehensive security program
- Regular risk assessment
- Employee training on data security
- Vendor management and oversight
- Incident response plan

3.2 STATE DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION LAWS
If personal information compromised:
- Notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay
- Notify state attorney general if >500 residents affected
- Notify consumer reporting agencies if >1,000 affected
- Provide free credit monitoring (some states)

Personal information includes:
- Social Security number
- Driver's license number
- Financial account numbers
- Credit/debit card numbers
- Health information

3.3 CYBERSECURITY REQUIREMENTS
New York DFS Cybersecurity Regulation (23 NYCRR 500):
- Appoint Chief Information Security Officer
- Implement cybersecurity program
- Conduct penetration testing and vulnerability assessments
- Audit trail for privileged accounts
- Multi-factor authentication
- Encryption of nonpublic information
- Annual board reporting

Other states adopting similar regulations.

CHAPTER 4: FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT (FCRA)

4.1 PERMISSIBLE USE OF CREDIT REPORTS
May obtain for:
- Underwriting insurance
- Rating insurance (if allowed by state)
- Claims settlement evaluation

Must have consumer's written consent.

4.2 ADVERSE ACTION REQUIREMENTS
If take adverse action based on credit report:
- Provide written notice to consumer
- Include name and contact of credit reporting agency
- State that agency didn't make decision
- Include notice of right to dispute
- Provide free copy of credit report

Adverse actions include:
- Denial of coverage
- Higher rates
- Reduced coverage limits
- Cancellation or non-renewal

4.3 CONSUMER RIGHTS
Must allow consumers to:
- Obtain free copy of credit report annually
- Dispute inaccurate information
- Place fraud alert or credit freeze
- Opt out of prescreened offers

CHAPTER 5: ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML)

5.1 BANK SECRECY ACT (BSA) COMPLIANCE
Insurance companies must:
- Implement AML program
- Designate AML compliance officer
- Conduct risk-based customer due diligence
- Monitor for suspicious activity
- File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- Maintain records for 5 years

5.2 CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION PROGRAM (CIP)
Verify identity of customers:
- Name, date of birth, address
- Identification number (SSN or EIN)
- Documentary verification (driver's license, passport)
- Non-documentary verification if necessary

Red flags:
- Cash payments over $10,000
- Unusual policy structures
- Frequent policy changes
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile

5.3 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs):
- File for cash transactions over $10,000
- Submit to FinCEN within 15 days

Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs):
- File for suspicious transactions regardless of amount
- Submit within 30 days
- Do not notify customer of SAR filing

CHAPTER 6: MARKET CONDUCT EXAMINATIONS

6.1 EXAMINATION PROCESS
State regulators conduct examinations:
- Routine: Every 3-5 years
- Target: When issues identified
- Multi-state: Coordinated by NAIC

Examination scope:
- Underwriting practices
- Rating and pricing
- Claims handling
- Producer compensation
- Complaint handling
- Advertising and marketing

6.2 DOCUMENT RETENTION
Maintain records for examination:
- Policy files: 7 years after closure
- Claim files: 7 years after closure
- Financial records: 7 years
- Board minutes: Permanently
- Rating and underwriting manuals: Current + 7 years
- Marketing materials: 7 years

6.3 EXAMINATION FINDINGS
Possible outcomes:
- No violations found
- Minor violations with corrective action plan
- Significant violations with fines and consent order
- License suspension or revocation (severe cases)

CHAPTER 7: ADVERTISING AND MARKETING COMPLIANCE

7.1 TRUTH IN ADVERTISING
Advertisements must not:
- Contain false or misleading statements
- Omit material information
- Use deceptive formatting or presentation
- Compare to competitors unfairly
- Use testimonials without proper disclosures

Required disclosures:
- Company name and contact information
- Policy limitations and exclusions
- Actual cost of coverage
- Terms and conditions
- "Not available in all states" if applicable

7.2 TELEMARKETING RULES
Comply with TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act):
- Obtain prior express written consent for calls/texts
- Maintain do-not-call list
- Honor do-not-call requests immediately
- Provide company identification in calls
- Call only during permitted hours (8 AM - 9 PM)

CAN-SPAM Act for emails:
- Clear opt-out mechanism
- Honor opt-outs within 10 days
- Accurate subject lines and sender information
- Include physical postal address
- Identify message as advertisement

CHAPTER 8: DISCRIMINATORY PRACTICES

8.1 REDLINING PROHIBITION
Cannot:
- Refuse coverage based on geographic location alone
- Charge higher rates in minority neighborhoods without actuarial justification
- Apply different underwriting standards by location
- Use ZIP code as sole determining factor

8.2 PROTECTED CLASSES
Fair Housing Act protections:
- Race and color
- National origin
- Religion
- Sex
- Familial status
- Disability

Additional state protections may include:
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity
- Marital status
- Age (in insurance context, must be actuarially justified)

8.3 DISPARATE IMPACT
Even neutral policies can violate law if:
- Disproportionately affect protected class
- Cannot be justified by business necessity
- Less discriminatory alternative exists

Regular testing required to ensure compliance.

CHAPTER 9: SOLVENCY REGULATION

9.1 CAPITAL AND SURPLUS REQUIREMENTS
Minimum requirements vary by state:
- Property/casualty insurers: $2-5 million
- Must maintain Risk-Based Capital (RBC) above action level
- Quarterly financial statements required
- Annual audited financial statements

9.2 INVESTMENT RESTRICTIONS
Limitations on:
- Single issuer exposure
- Asset classes (real estate, equities)
- Non-investment grade securities
- Foreign investments
- Affiliated company investments

Must maintain:
- Adequate liquidity
- Diversified portfolio
- Quality assets
- Appropriate asset-liability matching

9.3 GUARANTY FUND ASSESSMENTS
Member of state guaranty funds:
- Pay assessments when insurers become insolvent
- Typical assessment: 1-2% of premium
- May offset against future taxes
- Ensures payment to policyholders of failed insurers

CHAPTER 10: AGENT AND BROKER OVERSIGHT

10.1 PRODUCER CONDUCT
Agents/brokers must:
- Act in best interest of clients (fiduciary duty in some states)
- Provide suitable coverage recommendations
- Disclose all material information
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Maintain client confidentiality

10.2 COMMISSION TRANSPARENCY
Required disclosures (varies by state):
- Commission rates upon request
- Contingent commission arrangements
- Ownership interests in insurers
- Revenue sharing agreements

10.3 ERRORS AND OMISSIONS
Producers liable for:
- Failure to procure coverage as requested
- Inadequate coverage recommendations
- Failure to provide renewal notices
- Negligent advice or misrepresentation

Must maintain E&O insurance with minimum $1 million coverage.

CHAPTER 11: POLICY CANCELLATION AND NON-RENEWAL

11.1 PERMITTED REASONS
May cancel or non-renew for:
- Non-payment of premium
- Material misrepresentation on application
- Substantial increase in risk
- Suspension or revocation of license
- Physical changes to property creating increased hazard

11.2 NOTICE REQUIREMENTS
Advance notice required:
- Cancellation: 10-30 days (varies by state and reason)
- Non-renewal: 30-60 days before expiration
- Mid-term cancellation: Full premium refund pro-rata
- Non-renewal: No refund, policy expires

11.3 PROHIBITED REASONS
Cannot cancel or non-renew solely because:
- Filing of claims (except pattern of fraud)
- Age of insured (discriminatory)
- Residence in particular area (redlining)
- Exercise of legal rights
- Protected class status

CHAPTER 12: ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES

12.1 REGULATORY ACTIONS
Insurance departments may:
- Issue cease and desist orders
- Impose civil fines
- Suspend or revoke licenses
- Require corrective action plans
- Place company under supervision
- Order restitution to policyholders

12.2 CRIMINAL PENALTIES
Serious violations may result in:
- Criminal prosecution
- Imprisonment for executives
- Corporate fines
- Consent judgments

Examples: fraud, embezzlement, willful violations of law

12.3 PRIVATE CAUSES OF ACTION
Consumers may sue for:
- Breach of contract
- Bad faith claim handling
- Unfair or deceptive practices
- Fraud or misrepresentation
- Violation of consumer protection laws

Potential damages: compensatory, consequential, punitive, attorney fees

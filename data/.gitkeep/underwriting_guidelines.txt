UNDERWRITING GUIDELINES
Personal Lines Insurance
Revised: January 2024

SECTION 1: AUTO INSURANCE UNDERWRITING

1.1 DRIVER ELIGIBILITY
Acceptable risks:
- Licensed drivers age 18 and above
- Valid driver's license for minimum 1 year
- Clean driving record (no major violations in 3 years)

Unacceptable risks:
- DUI/DWI conviction within 5 years
- License suspension within 3 years
- More than 3 at-fault accidents in 3 years
- Reckless driving conviction within 3 years

1.2 VEHICLE ELIGIBILITY
Acceptable:
- Private passenger vehicles
- Light trucks and SUVs under 10,000 lbs
- Vehicles less than 15 years old
- Antique/classic cars (with special endorsement)

Unacceptable:
- Vehicles modified for racing
- Commercial vehicles without proper coverage
- Salvage title vehicles
- Exotic or ultra-luxury vehicles over $200,000

1.3 RATING FACTORS
Primary factors (weighted):
- Age of driver: 20%
- Driving history: 30%
- Vehicle type and value: 25%
- Annual mileage: 10%
- Geographic location: 10%
- Credit score: 5%

1.4 PREMIUM ADJUSTMENT RULES
Increase premium if:
- Any driver under age 25
- Sports car or high-performance vehicle
- Urban/high-theft area
- High annual mileage (>15,000 miles)
- Poor credit score

Decrease premium if:
- All drivers over age 25 with clean records
- Safety-rated vehicle with modern features
- Low mileage (<7,500 miles annually)
- Good credit score
- Multiple policies bundled

SECTION 2: HOMEOWNERS INSURANCE UNDERWRITING

2.1 PROPERTY ELIGIBILITY
Acceptable:
- Single-family homes, townhouses, condos
- Primary residences or secondary homes
- Properties in good condition
- Standard construction (wood frame, brick, concrete)

Unacceptable:
- Properties in FEMA flood zones without flood insurance
- Homes with unrepaired damage
- Properties with more than 2 claims in 3 years
- Vacant properties (vacant >60 days)
- Earth homes, dome homes, log homes (without special review)

2.2 PROPERTY INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS
Inspection required if:
- Home value exceeds $500,000
- Roof is older than 15 years
- Property has swimming pool
- Home is older than 40 years
- Switching from another insurer
- Prior claim history unknown

2.3 ROOF AGE GUIDELINES
- Roofs 0-10 years: Full replacement cost
- Roofs 11-15 years: Replacement cost with inspection
- Roofs 16-20 years: Actual cash value only
- Roofs over 20 years: Coverage declined (must replace roof first)

Exceptions for:
- Metal roofs: Add 10 years to thresholds
- Tile roofs: Add 10 years to thresholds
- Recently inspected/certified roofs

2.4 RISK ASSESSMENT
Increase premium for:
- Coastal properties (hurricane exposure)
- Homes with swimming pools
- Homes with trampolines
- Homes with wood-burning stoves
- High-value homes (>$750,000)
- Homes with flat roofs

Decrease premium for:
- Gated community
- Central station alarm system
- Impact-resistant roofing
- Hurricane shutters
- Sprinkler system
- New construction (<5 years)

SECTION 3: CREDIT-BASED INSURANCE SCORING

3.1 SCORE TIERS
Tier 1 (Excellent): 750+ credit score
- 20% discount from base rate
- Best coverage options available

Tier 2 (Good): 650-749 credit score
- Standard rates
- All coverage options available

Tier 3 (Fair): 550-649 credit score
- 15% surcharge
- Higher deductibles required

Tier 4 (Poor): Below 550 credit score
- 35% surcharge
- Limited coverage options
- May require manual underwriting approval

3.2 EXCEPTIONS TO CREDIT SCORING
Do not use credit score if:
- Extraordinary life circumstance (bankruptcy due to medical bills, divorce, identity theft)
- Insufficient credit history
- State law prohibits credit-based insurance scoring
- Applicant provides documentation of circumstance

SECTION 4: CLAIMS HISTORY EVALUATION

4.1 ACCEPTABLE CLAIMS HISTORY
- 0-1 claims in past 3 years: Standard rates
- 1 not-at-fault auto accident: No penalty
- 1 homeowners claim under $5,000: Minimal impact

4.2 UNACCEPTABLE CLAIMS HISTORY
- 3 or more claims in 3 years: Decline coverage
- 2 or more at-fault auto accidents: Decline coverage
- Large loss history (>$50,000 total): Decline coverage
- Any intentional damage claim: Permanent decline

4.3 CLAIM-FREE CREDITS
- 3 years claim-free: 5% discount
- 5 years claim-free: 10% discount
- 10 years claim-free: 15% discount

SECTION 5: SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

5.1 HIGH-VALUE ACCOUNTS
Homes valued over $1,000,000 require:
- Full appraisal within 12 months
- Detailed inventory of contents
- Security system with monitoring
- Regular maintenance records
- Separate coverage for fine arts, jewelry

5.2 BUSINESS USE
Home business coverage required if:
- More than $5,000 in business equipment
- Clients visit home regularly
- Products manufactured or stored
- More than one employee

5.3 ANIMAL LIABILITY
Unacceptable dog breeds:
- Pit bulls
- Rottweilers
- Doberman pinschers
- Wolf hybrids
- Presa canarios

Exception: May accept with additional liability coverage and documented training/certification.

Exotic animals (not covered):
- Venomous reptiles
- Large cats
- Bears
- Primates
- Any animal requiring special permit

SECTION 6: BINDING AUTHORITY

6.1 UNDERWRITER AUTHORITY LEVELS
Junior Underwriter:
- Policies up to $300,000 total insured value
- Standard risks only
- No exceptions to guidelines

Senior Underwriter:
- Policies up to $1,000,000 total insured value
- Can approve minor exceptions
- Refer major exceptions to management

Underwriting Manager:
- Unlimited authority
- Can approve major exceptions with documentation
- Final decision on declined risks

SECTION 7: DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

All new policies must include:
- Completed application with signatures
- Motor vehicle reports for all drivers
- Credit report
- CLUE report (Comprehensive Loss Underwriting Exchange)
- Photos of property (for homeowners)
- Prior insurance declarations page

Renewal policies require:
- Updated motor vehicle reports
- Confirmation of no material changes
- Current photos (every 3 years for homeowners)

SECTION 8: DECLINATION PROCEDURES

When declining coverage:
- Provide written notice within 10 days
- State specific reasons for declination
- Cite applicable guidelines or regulations
- Inform of appeal process
- Suggest alternative carriers if possible

Adverse action notice required if declined based on:
- Credit report
- CLUE report
- Motor vehicle report

Include information on how to obtain free copy of report and dispute inaccuracies.

CLAIMS HANDLING PROCEDURES
Document Version: 2.1
Last Updated: December 2023

CHAPTER 1: FIRST NOTICE OF LOSS (FNOL)

1.1 INITIAL CONTACT
When a claim is reported, the claims representative must:
- Record date and time of loss
- Obtain policy number and verify coverage is active
- Collect claimant contact information
- Document basic facts of the incident
- Assign claim number immediately

1.2 URGENCY CLASSIFICATION
Claims are classified into three urgency levels:
- Priority 1 (Critical): Total loss, serious injury, legal deadlines - respond within 2 hours
- Priority 2 (Urgent): Significant damage, moderate injury - respond within 24 hours
- Priority 3 (Standard): Minor damage, no injury - respond within 48 hours

CHAPTER 2: INVESTIGATION REQUIREMENTS

2.1 DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
All claims must include:
- Incident report with detailed description
- Police report (if applicable)
- Photographs of damage from multiple angles
- Witness statements (minimum 2 if available)
- Medical records (for injury claims)
- Repair estimates from licensed vendors

2.2 FRAUD INDICATORS
Red flags requiring enhanced investigation:
- Inconsistent statements from claimant
- Prior claims history showing pattern
- Damage inconsistent with described incident
- Late reporting (more than 7 days)
- Reluctance to provide documentation
- Claimant pressure for immediate payment

2.3 INVESTIGATION TIMELINE
- Initial assessment: Within 3 business days
- Complete investigation: Within 15 business days
- Final determination: Within 30 days of receiving all documentation

CHAPTER 3: COVERAGE DETERMINATION

3.1 POLICY REVIEW
Adjusters must verify:
- Policy was active on date of loss
- Type of coverage applicable
- Deductible amounts
- Coverage limits and exclusions
- Any endorsements or riders

3.2 LIABILITY ASSESSMENT
Determine percentage of fault using:
- Police reports
- Traffic laws
- Witness testimony
- Physical evidence
- Expert opinions (if needed)

Comparative negligence applies in most jurisdictions.

CHAPTER 4: SETTLEMENT PROCESS

4.1 DAMAGE VALUATION
For property damage:
- Obtain 2-3 repair estimates
- Use industry-standard pricing guides
- Consider actual cash value vs. replacement cost
- Account for depreciation on older vehicles

For total loss:
- Use market value from recent comparable sales
- Consider vehicle condition before loss
- Factor in mileage and options
- Subtract salvage value if applicable

4.2 PAYMENT AUTHORIZATION
Payment authority levels:
- Adjuster: Up to $10,000
- Senior Adjuster: Up to $50,000
- Claims Manager: Up to $250,000
- Director approval required: Over $250,000

4.3 PAYMENT PROCESSING
- Issue payment within 5 business days of settlement agreement
- Direct deposit preferred
- Check by mail if direct deposit unavailable
- Provide itemized settlement statement

CHAPTER 5: SUBROGATION

5.1 SUBROGATION IDENTIFICATION
Pursue subrogation when:
- Third party is at fault
- Recovery potential exceeds $2,500
- Clear liability and collectability

5.2 SUBROGATION PROCESS
- Reserve subrogation rights in settlement letter
- Send demand letter to at-fault party within 60 days
- File lawsuit if necessary within statute of limitations
- Recover claim payment plus expenses

CHAPTER 6: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

6.1 CLAIM DENIAL
Denials must include:
- Specific policy provisions supporting denial
- Clear explanation in plain language
- Information on appeal process
- Deadline for appeal submission

6.2 APPEALS PROCESS
Claimants may appeal within 30 days by:
- Submitting written appeal with new evidence
- Requesting independent appraisal
- Participating in mediation

6.3 ARBITRATION
If settlement cannot be reached:
- Binding arbitration available
- Each party selects one arbitrator
- Third arbitrator selected jointly
- Decision is final and legally binding

CHAPTER 7: QUALITY ASSURANCE

7.1 FILE REVIEW
Random file audits conducted quarterly to ensure:
- Complete documentation
- Timely processing
- Accurate valuations
- Proper payment authorization
- Compliance with regulations

7.2 CUSTOMER SATISFACTION
All claimants receive survey after claim closure. Target satisfaction score: 85% or higher.

CHAPTER 8: REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

8.1 STATE REGULATIONS
Claims handling must comply with:
- Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act
- State-specific prompt payment laws
- Privacy and data security requirements
- Anti-fraud reporting obligations

8.2 REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
Report to state insurance department:
- All claim denials (within 10 days)
- Suspected fraud (immediately)
- Complaints and appeals (monthly summary)

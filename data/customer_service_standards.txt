CUSTOMER SERVICE STANDARDS
Excellence in Insurance Service Delivery
Revised: October 2023

SECTION 1: SERVICE PHILOSOPHY

1.1 CORE PRINCIPLES
Our customer service is built on:
- Responsiveness: Quick, efficient service delivery
- Empathy: Understanding customer needs and concerns
- Transparency: Clear communication and honest information
- Empowerment: Authority to resolve issues at first contact
- Consistency: Uniform service across all channels

1.2 SERVICE PROMISE
We commit to:
- Answer calls within 60 seconds
- Respond to emails within 4 business hours
- Resolve issues on first contact when possible
- Follow up on unresolved issues within 24 hours
- Treat every customer with respect and dignity

SECTION 2: RESPONSE TIME STANDARDS

2.1 PHONE SERVICE
Target metrics:
- Answer rate: 90% of calls within 60 seconds
- Average handle time: 5-7 minutes
- First call resolution: 85%
- Abandon rate: Less than 5%
- Customer satisfaction: 4.5/5.0 or higher

Peak hours coverage:
- Monday-Friday: 8 AM - 8 PM local time
- Saturday: 9 AM - 5 PM local time
- Emergency claims line: 24/7/365

2.2 EMAIL AND ONLINE
Response standards:
- Acknowledgment: Within 2 hours
- Full response: Within 4 business hours
- Complex inquiries: Within 24 business hours
- Account changes: Processed within 2 business hours

2.3 CHAT SERVICE
Available during business hours:
- Initial response: Within 30 seconds
- Average chat duration: 8-10 minutes
- Concurrent chats per agent: Maximum 3
- Transfer rate: Less than 10%

2.4 SOCIAL MEDIA
Monitoring and response:
- Monitor mentions 24/7
- Respond to direct messages within 2 hours
- Public comments within 4 hours
- Complaints escalated immediately

SECTION 3: CALL HANDLING PROCEDURES

3.1 GREETING AND IDENTIFICATION
Proper greeting includes:
- Company name
- Agent name
- Offer of assistance
Example: "Thank you for calling [Company]. This is [Agent Name]. How may I help you today?"

3.2 VERIFICATION AND SECURITY
Before discussing policy details:
- Verify caller identity with 2 factors:
  * Policy number or social security number
  * Date of birth or address
- Never disclose information to unauthorized parties
- Offer to call back if verification cannot be completed

3.3 ACTIVE LISTENING
Demonstrate through:
- Avoid interrupting
- Take detailed notes
- Ask clarifying questions
- Paraphrase to confirm understanding
- Acknowledge emotions appropriately

3.4 PROBLEM RESOLUTION
Resolution steps:
1. Fully understand the issue
2. Explain what you will do to help
3. Set clear expectations on timeline
4. Take ownership of the issue
5. Follow through on commitments
6. Confirm resolution with customer

SECTION 4: INQUIRY TYPES AND HANDLING

4.1 POLICY INFORMATION REQUESTS
Common inquiries:
- Coverage details and limits
- Premium amounts and due dates
- Deductible information
- Beneficiary information
- Policy documents

Handle by:
- Accessing policy system immediately
- Providing clear, complete information
- Offering to email policy documents
- Explaining any changes or updates

4.2 BILLING INQUIRIES
Address questions about:
- Payment due dates
- Payment methods
- Late payment consequences
- Payment plan options
- Refund requests

Resolution:
- Access billing history
- Explain charges clearly
- Offer payment solutions
- Process payments if authorized
- Send confirmation of arrangements

4.3 CLAIMS SUPPORT
For claims inquiries:
- Status updates on open claims
- Documentation requirements
- Payment timing
- Adjuster contact information
- Appeal procedures

Action items:
- Provide specific status updates
- Never make promises on claim outcome
- Escalate to claims department if needed
- Document all customer concerns
- Set realistic expectations

4.4 POLICY CHANGES
Common change requests:
- Add/remove vehicles or properties
- Change coverage limits
- Update contact information
- Add/remove drivers
- Change beneficiaries

Processing:
- Verify effective date requested
- Explain premium impact
- Process immediately if authorized
- Send confirmation documentation
- Update all relevant systems

SECTION 5: DIFFICULT SITUATIONS

5.1 HANDLING COMPLAINTS
When customer is upset:
- Let them express frustration
- Apologize for their experience (not necessarily the outcome)
- Avoid being defensive
- Focus on solutions
- Escalate if cannot resolve

Empathy statements:
- "I understand why you're frustrated"
- "I would feel the same way in your situation"
- "Let me see what I can do to help"
- "I appreciate you bringing this to my attention"

5.2 CLAIM DENIALS
Explaining denials:
- Review policy language together
- Explain specific exclusion or limitation
- Provide written denial letter details
- Explain appeal process
- Offer additional assistance

Never:
- Argue with customer
- Be dismissive of their concern
- Blame underwriting or claims department
- Make promises that can't be kept

5.3 RATE INCREASES
When customer questions rate increase:
- Explain factors affecting premium
- Discuss available discounts
- Review coverage to ensure appropriate
- Offer to requote with different options
- Provide competitive shopping comparison if requested

5.4 ESCALATION PROCEDURES
Escalate to supervisor when:
- Customer specifically requests
- Issue cannot be resolved at current level
- Potential for lawsuit or regulatory complaint
- Policy exception required
- Customer extremely dissatisfied

Supervisor must:
- Call back within 2 hours
- Review entire interaction history
- Attempt resolution
- Document decision and rationale

SECTION 7: DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS

7.1 INTERACTION LOGGING
Every interaction must include:
- Date and time
- Customer contact information
- Nature of inquiry or issue
- Actions taken
- Promised follow-up
- Resolution or next steps

7.2 NOTE QUALITY
Notes should be:
- Clear and concise
- Free of jargon or abbreviations
- Objective (facts, not opinions)
- Sufficiently detailed for anyone to understand
- Properly spell-checked

7.3 SYSTEM UPDATES
Update all relevant systems:
- CRM with interaction details
- Policy system with any changes
- Billing system if payment discussed
- Claims system if claim mentioned
- Marketing preference if expressed

SECTION 8: COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

8.1 REGULATORY ADHERENCE
Must comply with:
- State insurance regulations
- Privacy laws (GLBA, state laws)
- TCPA for outbound calls
- CAN-SPAM for emails
- Fair Credit Reporting Act
- ADA accessibility requirements

8.2 PROHIBITED PRACTICES
Never:
- Make false or misleading statements
- Promise something not in the policy
- Discuss rates without proper authorization
- Provide tax or legal advice
- Discriminate based on protected classes
- Pressure customer into decisions

8.3 PRIVACY PROTECTION
Safeguard customer information:
- Verify identity before discussing account
- Secure all documents and screens
- Shred sensitive materials
- Use secure email for sensitive information
- Report any suspected data breaches immediately

SECTION 9: SPECIAL POPULATIONS

9.2 DISABLED CUSTOMERS

- TTY/TDD for hearing impaired

- Alternative communication methods
- Assistance with forms and applications

9.3 LIMITED ENGLISH PROFICIENCY
Language services:
- Spanish-speaking agents available
- Translation service for other languages
- Written materials in common languages
- Interpretation services on demand
